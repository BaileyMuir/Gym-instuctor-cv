You will find a list of definitions below for the terminology used.

  • Tempo - in this case, relates to the time you take to complete the motion, e.g. bicep curl. I gave two seconds to bring the weight towards your chest and 4 to lower it under control to complete one whole movement.
  • Sets - a group of repeated movements performed as part of a period of physical exercise, e.g. how we take a break after we complete a set amount of reps would equal one set of that exercise.
  • Reps - are used for saying the number of times a particular exercise should be repeated in a sports workout. "Rep" is short for repetition.
  • Time - relates to the amount of time you have to complete as many reps you can in the allocated time, e.g. hold a plank for a minute
  • Hypertrophy - refers to an increase in size, structure, or function. In the case used here, we refer to the increase in the size of the muscle fibres.
  • To fail - Refers to the number of times you can complete a full movement intill fail e.g. stop when you can nolonger push yourself up during a dip.
  • Rest - the time taken between each set to recuperate.
  • BB - is shorthand for Barbell
  • EZ - is shorthand for EZ bar
  • DB - is shorthand for Dumbbell
